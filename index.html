About Me

Hello, I’m JIANG Zhihan. I'm currently a final-year Ph.D. candidate at the Dept. of Electrical and Electronic Engineering, the University of Hong Kong, under the supervision of Prof. Edith C.H. NGAI, and a visiting student at the Dept. of Biomedical Informatics, Columbia University, hosted by Prof. Xuhai Orson Xu. I received the bachelor and master degrees in Computer Science and Technology from Xiamen University under the supervision of Prof. Longbiao CHEN.

My research lies at the intersection of Human-Computer Interaction (HCI), Data Visualization (VIS), and Ubiquitous Computing (UbiComp), with a focus on developing analytics systems to empower biomedical and pervasive healthcare through human-AI collaboration. By integrating artificial intelligence, multimodal data visualization & analysis, and wearable sensing techniques, I aim to support deep insights, effective clinical interaction, and evidence-based decision-making. My work addresses key challenges in Digital Health & Personalized Medicine, developing solutions tailored to both individual needs and broader public behaviors, ultimately shaping more responsive and interpretable systems that empower individuals and communities.

I always welcome the opportunity for research collaboration and I'm eager to explore potential research ideas. If you're interested, please feel free to drop me an email.
