About Me

Hello, I’m Zhihan Jiang. I'm currently a master student from Fujian Key Laboratory of Sensing and Computing for Smart City at Xiamen University, under the supervision of Prof. Longbiao CHEN. I received the bachelor degree in Computer Science and Technology from Xiamen University in 2018. My research spans a range of different topics, such as inferring traffic violation-prone locations in a city using large-scale vehicle trajectory and road environment data, optimize fog radio access networks using user mobility and traffic data, re-balancing bike stations based on city-wide bike demand prediction, etc.

Research Experience

Publications

  1. Inferring Traffic Violation-Prone Locations with Vehicle Trajectory and Road Environment Data [traffic violation visualization] [traffic sign visualization] [link]
    • Based on the large-scale vehicle trajectory data and road environment data, we proposed a comprehensive, and dynamic framework to infer traffic violation-prone locations in cities.
    • Zhihan Jiang, Longbiao Chen, Binbin Zhou, Jinchun Huang, Tianqi Xie, Xiaoliang Fan, Cheng Wang. iTV: Inferring Traffic Violation-Prone Locations with Vehicle Trajectory and Road Environment. IEEE System Journal, 2020 [Accepted].
  2. Understanding Urban Structures and Crowd Dynamics Leveraging Large-Scale Vehicle Mobility Data [pdf] [visualization]
    • In order to facilitate efficient urban planning and effective city management, we proposed a data-driven framework to understand the city functions of different areas, as well as the crowd dynamics moving around the city.
    • Zhihan JIANG, Yan LIU, Xiaoliang FAN, Cheng WANG, Jonathan LI, Longbiao CHEN. Understanding Urban Structures and Crowd Dynamics Leveraging Large-Scale Vehicle Mobility Data[J]. Frontier of Computer Science, doi: 10.1007/s11704-019-9034-z.
  3. BikeNet: Accurate Bike Demand Prediction Using Graph Neural Networks for Station Rebalancing [pdf]
    • In order to improve the quality of bike sharing systems, we proposed a two-phase framework to accurately predict city-wide bike demands and effectively rebalance bikes stations.
    • Ruiying Guo, Zhihan Jiang, Jingchun Huang, Jianrong Tao, Cheng Wang, Jonathan Li, Longbiao Chen*. BikeNet: Accurate Bike Demand Prediction Using Graph Neural Networks for Station Rebalancing. The 16th IEEE International Conference on Ubiquitous Intelligence and Computing (UIC’19), Leicester, UK, 2019.
  4. Data-Driven Bike Sharing System Optimization: State of the Art and Future Opportunities [pdf]
    • This paper specifically focus on the data-driven BSS optimization problem and provide a comprehensive literature review with future research opportunities.
    • Longbiao Chen, Zhihan Jiang, Jiangtao Wang, Yasha Wang. Data-Driven Bike Sharing System Optimization: State of the Art and Future Opportunities. The 2019 International Conference on Embedded Wireless Systems and Networks (EWSN’19), Beijing, China, 2019.

In Submission

  1. A Data-Driven Patrol Task Scheduling Framework based on Traffic Violation Hotspot Prediction
    • Based on the scattered traffic violations and road environment data, we propose a three-phase data-driven framework to accurately predict traffic violation hotspots in cities and dynamically schedule patrol tasks with maximal coverage of traffic violation hotspots under resource constraints.
    • Zhihan Jiang, Longbiao Chen, Binbin Zhou, Xiaojuan Ma, Mingfei Sun, Xiaoliang Fan, Cheng Wang. A Data-Driven Patrol Task Scheduling Framework based on Traffic Violation Hotspot Prediction.
  2. Optimizing Fog Radio Access Networks for 5G Leveraging User Mobility and Traffic Data [visualization]
    • In order to optimize the fog-computing-based radio access network (Fog-RAN), we proposed a two-phase data-driven framework to cluster base stations (RRHs) into communities and find optimal data processing unit (BBU) allocation schemes that maximize BBU utilization rate.
    • Longbiao Chen, Zhihan Jiang, Dingqi Yang, Thi-Mai-Trang Nguyen, Cheng Wang. Optimizing Fog Radio Access Networks for 5G Leveraging User Mobility and Traffic Data.
